Myths around COVID19

MYTH

Spraying alcohol or chlorine all over the body can kill new coronavirus.

FACT

No, In fact it can be harmful to your eyes, mouth etc. Alcohol and chlorine can disinfect surfaces, but only when they are used under appropriate recommendations.

MYTH

Pets can spread the new coronavirus.

FACT

At present, there is no evidence to show that pets such as dogs or cats can get this infection. However, it is always a good idea to wash your hands with soap and water after contact with pets.

MYTH

Eating garlic can help prevent infection with the new coronavirus.

FACT

Garlic has some antimicrobial properties. However, there is no evidence that it can protect people from the new coronavirus.

MYTH

Corona virus can spread through food items.

FACT

There is no evidence to date of viruses that cause respiratory illness being transmitted via food or food packaging. Coronavirus can't multiply in food; they need an animal or human host to multiply.

MYTH

Vitamin C supplements will stop you from cathing COVID-19.

FACT

Researchers have yet to find any evidence that vitamin C supplememnts can render people immune to COVID-19 infection.
